Government Leaders

Emmerson Mnangagwa, President

Born 15 September 1942, Emmerson Dambudzo Mnangagwa is a Zimbabwean revolutionary and politician who serves as the 3rd and current President of Zimbabwe since 24 November 2017. The president studied law at the University of Zambia and later at the University of London. He has held a series of senior cabinet positions including, Minister of State Security, Speaker of Parliament, Minister of Defence and First Vice-President to mention a few.

Constatine Chiwenga, Vice-President

Born 25 August 1956, Constantine Guveya Chiwenga is a retired Zimbabwean general currently serving as Vice-President of Zimbabwe since December 2017. He also serves as the Minister of Defence, Security and War Veterans, and as the Vice-President of the ruling ZANU-PF party since December 2017. He is the former Commander of the Zimbabwe Defence Forces and Commander of the Zimbabwe National Army.

Kembo Mohadi, Vice-President

Born 15 November 1949, Kembo Mohadi is one of two Vice-Presidents of Zimbabwe since 28 December 2017. He briefly served as the Minister of Defence, Security and War Veterans in 2017. Previously he was Minister of State for National Security in the President's Office from 2015 to 2017 and Minister of Home Affairs from 2002 to 2015.
